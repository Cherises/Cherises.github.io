第三章：人际交往

淡化的集体情感

所有人的集体情感都淡化了。在这里之后，或许他们都厌烦了再花时间交新朋友，或许是已经基本了解了全体人的各种关系。自分开以来，感情逐步疏远。因为在目前信息发达的社会，大部分人把精力放在了网络上，因此形成了一个个的小集体。他们只顾得把感情用在这些集体上，对于外人则没有时间去理会。

由于信息的发达，许多人都了解甚至精通情感和外交方面的问题，可以把一个人看得非常透彻，以此来为自己未来的交友做铺垫。然而，我认为这些都是没有用的。外交做得好固然可以使自己有一个好的立足之地，但同时又会减少自己的时间。

缘分也是如此，时而充满惊喜，时而充满恐惧。无论她是怎样看待我的，我都不太会在意。**“拿得起，放得下”**是我的原则，这才是真正的我。我需要思考的是，做什么才是有意义的，我几乎不会把时间浪费在感情上。即使我心里有喜欢的人，那也只不过是我的意念所决定的，我会克服这些问题。

人性的自私本质

为什么说人都是自私的？人们总是在公共场合以自己的立场做事、说话，很少顾及旁人，而每个人都是如此，所以就会产生矛盾，直到有人愿意退一步为止。

处世之道与言语的约束

最近我又明白了许多处世之道。我感到自己应该更适合其中的一种：到目前为止，我给自己定的约束是，在工作和学习中可以畅所欲言，因为讨论知识需要大量的交流；除此之外，除非遇到非常大的麻烦，我不会多说半句闲话。因为人各不同，我的想法和观点不可能适合每一个人，有时候我随口说出的一句话，就可能引起不必要的麻烦，我又何必如此呢？并且，在言语的过程中若提到精确的数字，也不应该直接说出。世事无常理，唯有继续努力。

逆境的角色与前进的动力

这个社会是强者胜任，弱者退出的社会，所以有坏人、有邪恶都是很正常的。既然它们形成了，就说明社会上有可以滋生它们的地方。为什么说这些事正常的呢？没有歧视，哪有前进的动力？没有压迫，怎么会有反抗？没有坏人，又怎么能够彰显好人的好呢？

挑战自我与跳出圈子

在各行各业，有太多的问题需要我们去思考。如果我们不去亲身经历，那么其中的某些事情可能我们永远也不会知道。挑战自我是每个人成长所必须的，但也因为其中存在着危险或其他因素，总有人不愿冒险。平凡的道路上总是人满为患，我就要与众不同。我们只有有了足够的阅历，才能以平常心看待这个世界。正如我之前所说，什么样的环境造就什么样的人，所以我们可以简单地根据每个人的不同表现，来推断他们所处的环境如何。

谨慎与自我认知

无论遇到什么样的人，从他们身上得出什么样的结论，都不要先表达出来，因为在许多情况下，我们无法直接得出最好的结论。有些人身在周围，心却远隔千里，也有些人恰恰相反。

我之前就说过，在人类的生活中，充满着随机性，但在这些无数的随机事件中，也隐约包含着一些规律。在我所看清的规律中，我认为这些规律大都是利己的，很少有真正利他的，即使是有利他，也只是为了得到更多的利己而已。所以我总结道：都是这样的无情。

当我们生活在一个并非完全由我们掌控的环境中，肯定会在某些时候彰显我们的地位，简单来说就是刷存在感。无论存在感有多少，人们在第一次相遇时都会表现出最佳状态，为了所谓的“第一印象”，说到底不还是为了以后更好的生活吗？人们啊，总是在被动中去显示自己无谓的存在感。

我之前不是讲过人类的两种状态吗？其中最低级的，也就是很难经过大脑思考就能做出来的事情，其中之一就是模仿，尤其是盲目的模仿。看着别人很有主见地做一件事，然后自己就去做同样的事情，无论是好的还是坏的事情，别人都会做，这是再简单不过的事情了。就我个人而言，经常有人模仿我的事情，无论我在哪里，这也更加印证了我的做法是正确的。真正能够走得长远的，很少是能够模仿出来的。

现在回归正题。人们在相对陌生的环境下会做一些事情，也许是经常做的，也许是难得一见的事情。其目的之一就是能够得到被帮助的人的信赖，或是获得某种地位。我也承认这种方法很好用，但是盲目地去帮助往往得不到好的结果。其中之一就是被帮助的人的状态。对于我来说，我可以去帮助其他人，但我不求回报，只希望看到被帮助的人有被我帮助的样子，而不是理所应当。我最恨的就是被帮助的人不知好歹，把我的帮助当作理所应当。这样的人，我很难再继续帮助他们。一直以来，都是如此。

认知高度与社会现实

不知道我曾经说过没有，那就是人类社会永远逃脱不掉可以从动物社会追根溯源的这种方法。人与人交往是必不可少的一个环节，但我也从中看到了一些让我不可思议的地方，以及所谓的“垃圾环境”到底是什么样子的。

在高二时，我悟出了**“认知高度”**这个道理。但现在，或许我身边的人，仍在浑浑噩噩地浪费着自己的生命。前段时间我还总结道：一部分吸烟的人之所以吸得这么自在，是因为他根本不知道吸烟有害健康！这个荒唐的事情，就发生在我身边。有些人不要只看外表，你以为她看起来很文静，殊不知背地里做了多少见不得人的事情，或者有多大的反差。

另一方面，在底层的人反而对知识没有那么崇敬，更关注的是人际关系。其实这很符合常理，因为学又学不过别人，只能摆烂。而且话说回来，在我原本的心目中也觉得学习才是重要的、是受人尊敬的，但是来到了大职院才发现，并不是这样。非常现实的是，学习永远没有人际关系好使。他们只是为了应付所有人而制定了规则，只是为了约束。所谓的合格率、良品率，也都是被把控好的。

简单来说，只要有人，就不缺有人学习。如此一来，就可以顺理成章地用这些人来安排自己的人了。自己的人听自己的话，一切也尽在自己掌握，不用担心自己的位置被替代。反而是那些自己爬上来的人才是最危险的。我称之为**“只要保证底层稳定，上层随便乱，没有规则”**，这也就是为何底层人如此之多的原因。

国家就像一个巨大的养殖场，政府就是农场主，无论是时不时收获的鸡蛋，还是偶尔宰一头牛羊，总而言之，政府的收益永远是最高的。要想保证底层不乱，就要保证他们知道的信息有限，让他们只知道如何爱国，如何奉献，如何安稳地过日子。只要每天勤勤恳恳的，就可以有吃不完的粮食。只要遵守这套规则，你就可以安稳一辈子。

但是人人都有上进的想法，都想要更好的生活，怎么办？没问题。首先告诉你，一夜暴富没有捷径，除非好好学习，考上好的大学，成为国家**“打工人”**的一分子，为国家打工，这是其中一条专门为听话的人安排的方法。那些脑子聪明一点又听话的，基本上会走这条路，成为国家基层事业单位的打工人。因为听话，所以你的工资很稳定。

那么对于不听话、脑子好使的，一般不会去学习，而是早早地去社会上打工或者打拼。这些人就看运气了，如果能够碰到有缘人指点迷津还好，否则也只能被困在底层。

Chapter 3: Interpersonal Relationships

The Fading of Collective Bonds

People’s feelings for groups have grown distant. After this point, maybe they’re tired of spending time making new friends, or perhaps they’ve already grasped the relationships within the entire group. Since parting ways, feelings have slowly faded. In today’s information-rich society, most people focus their energy on online communities. They form their own groups and reserve their emotions for those connections, neglecting outsiders. With the rise of information, many people are well-versed in emotional and social issues, able to see through others to pave the way for future friendships. Yet, in my opinion, this is pointless. While being good at diplomacy can secure a good position for you, it also consumes your time.

Relationships are full of unexpected joy and fear. No matter how someone sees me, I won’t care too much. My principle is to be able to pick something up and put it down. This is the real me. The only thing I need to figure out is what is truly meaningful. I barely waste time on emotions, and even if I have someone I like, that’s just a decision made by my mind, and I will overcome these issues.

The Selfish Nature of Humanity

Why is it said that everyone is selfish? People always act and speak from their own standpoint in public, rarely considering others. Since everyone does this, conflicts inevitably arise until someone is willing to take a step back.

The Art of Restrained Communication

I’ve learned many things about how to conduct myself. I believe I am best suited for a specific approach. I’ve set a personal rule for myself: in work and study, I can talk freely because discussing knowledge requires extensive communication. Outside of that, unless I face a significant problem, I won’t say an unnecessary word. This is because everyone is different, and my thoughts and opinions may not suit everyone. Sometimes, a casual remark can cause unnecessary trouble, so what’s the point? Furthermore, when speaking, if a precise number is mentioned, it shouldn’t be stated directly. There is no absolute right or wrong in this world; you must keep working hard.

The Role of Adversity in Society

This society is one where the strong survive and the weak are eliminated. Therefore, it is normal to have bad people and evil. The existence of these things indicates that society provides an environment for them to form. Why are they considered normal? Without discrimination, where would the motivation to progress come from? Without oppression, how could there be resistance? Without bad people, how could the good of good people be highlighted?

Embracing Challenges and Avoiding Conformity

In every industry, there are countless problems to consider. If we don’t go through them, we may never understand certain things. Challenging ourselves is a necessary part of personal growth. But because of the risks or other factors involved, some people are unwilling to take the risk. The road to mediocrity is always crowded, and I must be different. Only with enough experience can we view the world from an ordinary perspective. As I said before, the environment shapes the person, so we can infer what a person’s environment is like from their behavior.

The Importance of Prudence and Self-Awareness

No matter what kind of person you meet or what conclusions you draw from them, do not express them immediately. In many cases, we cannot draw the best conclusion directly. Some people may be physically present but mentally far away, while others are the opposite.

I have said before that human life is full of randomness, but within these countless random events, there are also patterns. From what I have observed, most of these patterns are self-serving with very little benefit to others. Even when others are helped, it is only to gain a greater benefit for oneself. From this, I conclude that people are naturally unfeeling. When we live in a life that we cannot fully control, we will inevitably assert our status at some point, or simply “make our presence known.” People will display their best side when they first meet. Why? Isn’t it for a better life in the future? People are always passively showing off their presence.

I’ve talked about the two states of humanity before, and the lowest level is doing things with little thought. One such situation is imitation, especially blind imitation. People see someone doing something with conviction and then do the same, regardless of whether it’s good or bad. It’s a simple phenomenon. People often imitate me, which proves that what I’m doing is correct. However, what can truly go far is rarely something that can be imitated.

Now, back to the main point. People in relatively unfamiliar environments might do things they often do or things they rarely do. One of the reasons is to gain the trust or status of those they help. I admit this method is very effective, but blindly helping others often doesn’t lead to a good outcome. The attitude of the person being helped is a key factor. When I help someone, I don’t ask for a reward, but I at least hope they show gratitude. I hate it when people take my help for granted. I find it difficult to continue helping such people. It has always been this way.

I don’t know if I’ve said this before, but human society can always be traced back to animal society. Interacting with others is an essential part of being human, but I’ve also seen some incredible things in it and learned what a so-called “toxic environment” is like.

Class and the Pursuit of Knowledge

In my second year of high school, I came to an understanding of “cognitive altitude.” But even now, some people around me might still be wasting their lives in a daze. A while back, I concluded that some people who smoke so casually do so because they don’t know that smoking is bad for their health! This absurd situation happens right around me. Some people shouldn’t be judged by their appearance. You may think someone seems gentle, but you don’t know how many shameful things they’ve done behind the scenes or what a huge contrast exists.

On the other hand, people in lower social strata are not as reverent of knowledge and focus more on interpersonal relationships. This is quite logical. If they can’t outlearn others, they simply give up. I used to believe that studying was most important and worthy of respect. However, after coming to this vocational college, I’ve realized that’s not the case. The truth is that good interpersonal skills are always more effective than good grades.

Rules are only made to manage people and to restrict them. The so-called pass rates and good product rates are all controlled. Simply put, as long as there are people, there will be someone willing to study. This allows those in power to place their own people in positions of control. These trusted individuals are obedient, ensuring that everything is under control and their positions are never threatened. I call this the “as long as the bottom remains stable, the top can be in chaos without rules” principle. This is why there are so many people at the bottom. They are like a large farm for the country, and the government is the farmer. Whether it’s the eggs they collect or the occasional cow or sheep they slaughter, the government’s profit is always the highest.

To keep the bottom stable, their access to information must be limited. They are taught only about patriotism, dedication, and living a peaceful life. As long as they work hard every day, they will have plenty of food. If they follow this set of rules, they can have a stable life forever.

But everyone wants to improve and have a better life. How can they achieve that? The first thing they’re told is that there’s no shortcut to getting rich overnight unless they study hard, get into a good university, and become part of the country’s workforce. This is a path specifically designed for obedient people. Those who are smart and obedient will generally take this route, becoming civil servants in grassroots institutions. Because they’re obedient, their salaries are stable.

As for the disobedient and clever people, they usually won’t study and will enter the workforce or start their own businesses early. Their fate depends on luck. If they are lucky enough to meet a kind person who shows them the way, that’s great. Otherwise, they will also be trapped at the bottom.
