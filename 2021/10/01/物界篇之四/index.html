第二章：周围环境

竞争与自我保护

无论何时何地，只要有人群的地方，就必然会产生矛盾。为了资源，为了土地，为了面子，人们争来争去，对我来说一点意思都没有。虽然人类的一些本能，如争吵、嘲讽、打架等，不太适合现在的文明环境，但它们在特殊时期是真的有用。我本想与世无争，你们争你们的，可现在却发现，我不争别人的，别人就会来抢我的，认为我弱小好欺负，把我当做一个弱者。既然如此，如果我不争，就什么也不争；一旦要争，我就要争最好的。

动荡年代的生存法则

目前，人类最低的生活保障就是活着。如果连命都没有了，那一切也就不存在了。所以在往年的疫情中，我看到了：医生是必不可少的，粮食是必不可少的，通信也是必不可少的。在关键时刻，没有什么比用自己的工作来保命这件事更好了。通信则让我们坐在家里，可以尽知天下事，从而为我们的未来做好充分的准备。还有，未来一定要有自己的车子，像房车那种，可以做到想走就走，想停就停。未来的时代我不知道会发生什么，但是我会做好充分的准备，应对未来的危机。俗话说：靠天靠地不如靠自己，和平年代靠国家，动乱年代靠自己。

在动乱的年代中，我们千万不能随便挑战人性。就我写的这些内容来说，也仅仅是为了保全自己。在人类当中，做好自己的角色，别人也应该如此。成长本来就是要一次次地突破，同时在突破前要充分了解自己的情况，并默默地告诉自己，杀不死我的，必将使我更加强大。

历史的束缚与理性的挣脱

今天我们来聊一聊**“人生历史”**。我相信每一个人都不会陌生，更不会轻易忘记。历史真是个好东西，它由我们产生，由我们记住，然后在未来的某一瞬间，全部绽放在我们的脑海中。这些可以说和情感是同一类东西，会左右我们的理性思维。在我们的历史中，也许我们犯过严重的错误，也许做过令人匪夷所思的事情，也许是几件难以忘记的事情。总之，有些记忆对于我们来说是那么美好，我们总是沉浸在那种感觉中，回味无穷。我也是这样的人。

可是，我的理性认知告诉我，这些都不应该出现在我的未来生活中，换句话说，这会影响我们的正常行为判断，甚至会阻碍前进。我之前有一种说法是：随着人们逐渐长大成熟，经历的多了，慢慢就会形成一种定性的行为，就像是函数的参数一样。函数里面都是放好的行为，只要外面的参数一变，我们就会跟着做出相应的反应。如此说来好像没有什么好奇怪的，但是真的是如此吗？一旦我们的认知固化，若是想要改变，那就很难了。

历史对于我来说，那只能是放在它该放的地方，那就是历史产生的地方。我们不用去回想，除非有必要。如果地方消失了，那历史也应该跟着它消失。没有感情才是我最好的状态。

消费主义与人性的弱点

利用人性来赚钱。因为他们太了解普通人了，很容易就能抓住人们的心理，然后去利用，灌输各种思想。他们会用道德来捆绑，把心意和金钱绑定，比如“第一杯奶茶”、“你的舍长/班长给你买了……吗？”。其实，一般发布这些内容最多的还是商家而已，一切的一切都是为了他们自己获利。

在平常的生活中，我们会遇到很多让我们感到方便的事情，可是这些所谓的方便只不过是他们可以更快地去获利。所以前提是你得有钱，不然寸步难行。在很多时候，我们应该自己成为主导，而不是被当作韭菜来割。人人都知道在社会上要保护好自己，可实际情况下还是容易吃亏，总是成为一个受害者。

比如平常我们遇到各种非常热情的销售者，其实这很容易和身旁与你无关的路人比一比，真的是销售者本身的热情吗？你对他们说你现在没钱试试，我很确定大部分人都会给你脸色看。所以呢？还要什么感情呢？我将你的热情好客当作好心，而你仅仅是为了我口袋里的钱。你要想销售出你的商品，那么你就必须做出相应的工作来卖掉你的产品。因此，以上的热情好客都只是他们的工作而已。而对于我们来说，一手交钱一手拿货，不说一句废话。

观点与认知

谁觉得自己是对的，那它自己就是对的。

要经常地站在各个角度看待环境和问题，得出一个趋于平稳的观点。

这世界上永远没有安逸的环境，没有让你白占的便宜。永远不变的就是：你选择安逸，就走上了死亡的道路。

我现在终于清楚了，那就是没有对比就没有伤害，没有压力就没有动力。我一直想着不要什么事情都要和别人比，但是我却忽略了一个非常严重的问题，就是如果我不和别人比，我就很难有动力去学习。环境真的是很重要，基本上所有的人都是在对比和竞争中获得成长的。以后，我需要经常和别人对比了。对于现在的我来说，以及现在的环境，的确是太安逸了。即使我有志向，也很难有所成就。因为现在我很容易在娱乐的世界中迷失自我。

身体本能与自我提升

我现在在想一个问题，就是当人们想要去做一件事的时候，和真正去做一件事的时候，好像不是由我们的意志所决定的。举个例子：你现在想要去学习，首先会在大脑中简单地描绘一下学习的开始过程，但却很难去行动。身体会先不由自主地等一会儿，直到我们的理性认为学习的时间很少了，或者是不学习不行了的时候，我们才会去做我们想要做的事情。

虽然这种情况常见到已经被我们的意志所融合了，我们会很自觉地认为是我们本身不想早早地开始，想要能多休息一会儿是一会儿。除非想到非常紧急的事情，我们才会直接跳起来去工作。这也许是身体的一种本能吧，毕竟我们的身体每动一下都需要能量，能少消耗一点是一点。

我们可以用最少的时间学到我们大部分时间都学不到的东西。换句话说，我们学习的过程中只有那么一小点时间是高效的，其余的都是在充数，或者说是在休息。我们人类的肉体和精力都是有限的。与其花费大量的时间去透支它们，倒不如用所有时间中状态最好的时候去用来提升自己，也就是学习。其余的时间合理分配。当然，其余的时间不是休息就是休息。

大脑是个好东西，它可以想到任何东西。人存活于世界上，身体必须要听从某一个方向。谁的决定能够活下去，就听谁的。这也是为什么早期有低级中枢支配，成熟后由大脑支配的原因。谁不想要自己多得一分利呢？谁不想多享受一点呢？

阶级固化与教育的本质

昨天我看到了这样一个视频，场景是在一所技校里面。原因是有同学公然在教室里打电话，影响到了老师讲课，所以老师非常生气，并指责这位学生。我看完这则视频后，心头不由得一震。我们都知道技校里面是什么情况，应该是全县最差的学生才会在里面，也是无缘高中的一部分学生。我们也知道，学校就是在让一代又一代的孩子分成三六九等。成绩好的在一起，成绩差的在一起。这似乎非常符合社会的价值观，每个人也都是这么认为的。

问题之一就是，首先国家筛选下来的人由于知识技能差，所以对国家的用处不大，因此根本就不在国家的用心范围之内。换句话说就是把被筛选出来的人往那一扔，让他们自生自灭去吧。然而我们还知道，环境是最容易影响一个人的。这样一来，即使是成绩差但素质还行的学生，基本上从技校出来以后也就没有什么素质了。因为凡是成绩差的，基本上对国家没有一点用。

国家的真正目的是什么？选拔人才，为了国家发展壮大，需要科技，像数学、航天之类的科学家。但是人民的目的仅仅是需要好好地生活下去，需要的仅仅是过好日子，谁会在家里搞航天科技呢？这在一定程度上，两者并不是一条路上的。但是呢，国家是有权力和资本的，它当然有能力改变这种情况，那就是大力扶持人才选拔机构，也就是学校。并且慢慢地把学校变成一代又一代年轻人唯一的出路（这当然还是人民群众的“功劳”，国家仅仅只是说了：只要你学习被我们录用，你就不用干体力活了，还有很多工资。这是必然的，你为国家贡献了，不给你利益才怪呢）。

于是，利用人们的“聪明、盲目”等各种弱点，果然，人人都向往着改变命运，渐渐地这也成了一种常态，也就是成为了一种风俗：孩子一出生，考大学的道路就被安排好了。哎，这只不过是一种政策而已啊，先不说是不是双赢。

所以无论未来发展成什么状态，首先有一点我们不能忘记，那就是：我们的命运是由我们自己掌握的，不是所谓的学校。 任何一个地方都不是我们永久待下去的。不要被骗了，不要被蒙蔽了双眼。

情感的束缚与现实的残酷

我们永远都不会知道，在某一个瞬间，某一个时刻，有千万种回忆和情感涌现在我们脑海中，来不及感慨，或者是在沉默，不想说话，甚至不想动一下。知道吗？这些就是情感的束缚，人是无法避免的。人生啊，就是在不断地向前奔跑。有时也会回头看看，成长过程中，以前的我们是什么样子。是以泪洗面，还是嘶底呐喊，还是波澜不惊。记忆总是要被历史磨灭的。

流量经济与价值内卷

这几天我一直在看抖音，现在突然发现了一件非常有趣的事情，那就是现在拍视频的人应该很清楚什么是流量，也就是什么是大多数人喜欢看的，拍这样的视频就可以得到很多的流量，也就是收视率。现在很多类型的视频都开始有着共同点，那就是（女生）。我不知道是不是因为男性同胞的热爱。举个例子，抖音上其中之一是电脑行业的，做装机评测之类的。本来玩电脑并不是女生的特长，大部分都是男生群体，可后来呢，受流量的影响，慢慢地女生开始加入这个行业，说是加入，倒不如说是男生更喜欢在电脑旁边加一个美女。出镜的女生基本上长得都很漂亮，甚至穿着JK水手服，这种广大男生都喜欢的。所以说这渐渐地成为了这个行业的内卷了。既然又回到了我之前所提到的问题上，那后果我就不用多说了。这些外在的、容易模仿的间接性价值，最终会让制作者越来越累。可观看者就有眼福了。

我现在可以预测未来的情况，那就是只要是有需求的事物，都会游走在法律的边缘。呵！

政治利益与人性需求

广义上可以分为经济利益、生态利益、社会利益、民众利益、文化利益、政治利益等。拿政治利益来说，民众除了需要钱，还需要权力和权利。在满足基本生存之后，老百姓还想要过上更有尊严、更有面子的生活，这就是对权利的追求。权力和权利加在一起就是政治利益。实际上，公权力和私权力也就是政治上的两个核心关键词。

我们会在能够满足个体基本生存需要之后保护他人，甚至于在我们能够接受的尺度内牺牲个人利益。每个人物质需求和精神需求的标准不一样，往往越聪明的人越倾向于后者，因为他们得到物质利益相对容易。同时，这也解释了为什么个人能力那么重要，因为它还能决定你社会贡献的高度和道德层次的高度。连自保都做不到的人，他口中的爱不就是虚伪或者妄言吗？有人牺牲物质利益换取精神利益，有的人牺牲精神利益换取物质利益。

出于生存本能产生的与外界（自我以外）的斗争，会让人们的社会性受损。我们的精神世界会倾向于修罗道，孤独、警戒，失去信任感、安全感，夜不能寐，枕下藏刀，并最终迫使我们失去生存意志。

资本陷阱与“打工人”的困境

所谓的典型劳动工人的基本形态（也就是最简单的大众思想）：自己做工作是为了得到金钱，然后用金钱去换取自己想要的商品，只要安安稳稳地走过这一生。对于这类人在资本社会中是很难有自由的，因为他们的思想简单，很容易被利用。在正常的情况下，可以很容易地被老板用更多的报酬换取他们的自由。这类人做的往往是大众岗位，不可或缺但又供不应求的工作。通过利益的收买，可以很容易地被迫工作。而这类人往往很难有固定的存款，原因不仅仅是因为薪水少，再加上社会的各种诱导，比如所谓的“年轻人的第一台设备”之类的，各种广告各种洗脑，再加上利用人们的各种心理活动（换句话说，他们那点小心思早已被大数据拿捏得死死的）。

社会唯一的目的还是换取你们身上的钱，然后你们只能为了生活而被迫工作。在这样一个死循环中，可能是现在大部分年轻人的现状。总的来说，社会通过各种方式掏空你们的钱，而你们被迫给社会工作。而对于“受害者”得到的是什么呢？整天吃泡面为了买一部 iPhone 手机？为了拿到名牌包包？为了在朋友圈中炫耀自己又去了星巴克？总之，还是为你们那点小心思买单。

保持清醒与认清现实

我非常赞同的一点就是不干扰别人的生活。每个人都有自己的生活轨迹，为了热爱，为了感觉，为了理想，而我们自己所认为的也仅仅是我们自己认为的。如果每个人都用自己所知道的去评价别人，那只会让情况更糟。所以不必评价他们，默默地看好他们即可，做好自己的事情就行了。

保持清醒的头脑才能很好地在社会中立足，而保持清醒所付出的代价可不是谁都能够承受的。其实对于人来说，最简单也是最基本的需求就是能够好好地活下去。但这显然是在没有弄清事实之前所说的话。人们的需求什么时候满足过？要不是你被逼无奈，你会说那样的话？人们的需求是永远都满足不了的，得到了一点后还会去想得到更多的。这不也正是人类的缺点之一吗？当然，社会就是这样发展起来的。无论过程怎么样，总是被人们维持了一个相对稳定的状态。只要有利益，大家就可以一视同仁；只要你有利益，我们就可以交朋友。

现在谁都不能说这个社会怎么样，首先说这些话的都是无权无利、处于弱势的人。这个社会本身就是一个巨大的竞争平台。就像历史总是胜利者书写的一样，无论是人还是国家，谁不想美言自己几句，然后对失败者吐几口口水呢？这个社会总是要竞争的，而竞争的结果就是总要有一方要输。输并不可怕，可怕的是你认输啊！

一年一度的秋天马上就要到了，天气也慢慢凉了起来，又到了那个季节。每到这个季节，我的心情总是非常低沉，阴沉沉的天空，冰冷的世界。

在日常生活中，我们不应该随随便便地去帮助别人，应当首先分析这个人值不值得我们去帮助。有些人帮助了他们之后会感激不尽，而有些人如同填无底洞，永远填不满不说，最终你还会发现自己太傻，他们把你的帮助当作了理所应当。因为每个人都有不同的想法，而想法的背后是由思想高度来支撑的。因此，前面所说的后者当然不能说他们的行为是坏的、不可改变的，也许在未来的某一天，他们会意识到，但至少目前为止，在这些只想占便宜却不想付出的人中，应该不会有太多的人愿意和他们生活在一起。

不要随便地去生气，也同样要分析自己值不值得生气。如果你走在大街上，一个地痞流氓看你不顺眼骂了你几句，怎么，难道你也要生个气骂回去吗？虽说**“以其人之道还治其人之身”**，但我觉得更应该适用于那些不是普通人，想一想就让别人知道是什么意思的人。不要对牛弹琴，好的方法应该用于懂得这个方法的人。不要在俗人之中扮演自命清高，再清高有什么用？除了被歧视还是被歧视。

无论处于什么地位，都有太多的被动和太少的主动。我们所做的一切事情，说是为了自己，可最终还是为了别人的。房子再大，你顶多住一辈子；车子再好，也不过是坏了再修。平房是住，别墅也是住；自行车能跑，豪车也能跑，有什么区别呢？有人冒险一生，活在刀尖上，每天过着紧张的生活，他觉得这样的生活有意义；也有人平平淡淡，一日三餐，结婚生子，养老送终，他也觉得自己生活得很有意义。所以到底什么才是有意义的生活呢？答案就是：谁觉得自己的生活有意义，那就是最有意义的生活。 无论什么样的制度、环境条件、生活水平等一切可以拿来评说的事情，都败给了别人一句“我乐意”。是啊，只要人人“乐意”，那还有什么公平不公平，残忍不残忍呢？还有什么理由去改变他们呢？

爱的真相与自我保护

其实，除了自己家庭或家族的感情是最真实的以外，我们在社会上所遇到的绝大多数感情都是虚假的。这时候会有人问，那既然如此，为何我总和父母聊不来，和朋友之间却玩得很开心？这种现象不恰好和你说的相反吗？曾经有人解释过这种现象，大概说的是，父母的爱是无偿的，而朋友之间是有偿的。也有人说，我们对父母表现出的不爱，正是因为我们首先知道父母是爱我们的，这是无偿的，所以我们基本上都会选择无视和浪费。但朋友之间就不同了，刚开始彼此都不了解，我们不知道对方是否真心对待我们，于是很多人都会非常珍惜对方给予自己的感情。呵呵。

我们总想着用微笑面对这个世界，让自己最美好的一面展示给世界，可是最终我们却发现，我们越是表现得善良，就越是容易被别人看成是好欺负，或者对我们指手画脚。在我看来，任何人的美丽展现，首先都得有那个美丽展现的条件，会好好地保护自己，在这样的基础上才能更好地展现自己。当然，想得更深一点，在这个社会上，真的有绝对的保护吗？估计可能会有个别的例子。我觉得大部分人都是在被保护的过程中，会受到一些小的伤害，然后为了就是不受外界巨大的伤害，或者用自己的自由换取未来的安稳。这些就是社会法则，要么有人甘愿吃亏，要么一起平等互利，要么单方面地谋取暴利。

如果这个社会上人人都不吃亏，那么这个社会就不会稳定。所以还是那句话，自己的幸福生活都是建立在我们的痛苦之上的。这个“我们”包括地球上所有的生物，因为指不定你就会伤害了谁，比如吃一只猪，或者是一只小龙虾。当前的世界是什么样子的，大家都很清楚，**“电信诈骗”**相信大家都知道，越来越多的套路是层出不穷，站在利益的角度，从来都不顾别人受影响的后果。成年人的话影响可能不是很大，毕竟认知在那里。患难见真情，大事见人心，我觉得这就是人类社会吧，一点都不奇怪。所以，还是那句话，世界之大什么样的人都有，至少对我们而言，见到这些人时，不必惊慌，不必害怕，该依附的依附，该远离的远离。

裙摆效应与环境适应

女人在人类社会上起的作用真的是很奇怪的。之前不是有人说是裙摆效应吗？说的是在一段时间内，女性裙摆越短，则说明当前社会经济水平越高，反之则相反。总之，世界还是平衡的，任何现象的出现，必然会有一个开始的原因，至少在正常发展的情况下是这样。

肯定有很多人认为，一个人如果越容易适应社会，那么他便可以更好地活下去。可是换句话说，那他是否更容易被环境改变原有的样子？这样去想的确是很可怕，因为这类人能否成功，完全取决于他所处的环境。出生在恶劣环境下的孩子和书香世家的孩子，未来结果肯定不会一样。而如果孩子小时候的性格比较顽固，这何尝不是一种自我保护的反应呢？

Chapter 2: The Surrounding Environment

Competition and Self-Preservation

Wherever there are people, there will inevitably be conflict. For resources, for land, for face—they argue and fight, which I find completely meaningless. While some human instincts, like quarreling, mocking, and fighting, aren’t well-suited for our current civilized environment, they are truly useful in specific situations. I originally wanted to live a life of peace, letting others fight their battles. But now I’ve realized that if I don’t contend for what’s mine, others will take it, seeing me as weak and easy to bully. So, if I don’t fight, I fight for nothing. But if I’m going to fight, I’ll fight for the best.

Survival in Turbulent Times

What is the lowest form of human survival? It’s simply to be alive. If you don’t have life, nothing else matters. During the past years of the pandemic, I saw that doctors, food, and communication were all essential. At critical moments, there is nothing better than using your work to save your own life. Communication allows us to stay home and know everything happening in the world, preparing us for the future. Also, in the future, it’s crucial to have your own vehicle, like an RV, so you can go wherever and whenever you want. I don’t know what the future holds, but I’ll be fully prepared to handle any crisis. As the saying goes, relying on heaven and earth is not as good as relying on yourself. In times of peace, rely on the country; in times of turmoil, rely on yourself.

In chaotic times, we must not recklessly challenge human nature. The things I’ve written are merely for my own self-preservation. Within humanity, we must fulfill our roles, and others should do the same. Growth is a process of continuous breakthroughs, but before you break through, you must fully understand your own situation and silently remind yourself: what doesn’t kill you will make you stronger.

The Shackles of History and the Release of Reason

Let’s talk about “personal history.” I believe everyone is familiar with this concept and won’t easily forget it. History is a wonderful thing. It’s created by us, remembered by us, and in a future moment, it all blossoms in our minds. These memories are like emotions; they can sway our rational thinking. In our history, we may have made serious mistakes or done incredible things. Some memories are so beautiful that we love to indulge in them. I am this kind of person.

However, my rational mind tells me that these things shouldn’t be a part of my future life. In other words, they can affect our judgment and even hinder our progress. I once said that as people grow older and gain more experience, they slowly form a certain set of behaviors, like parameters in a function. The function’s actions are pre-determined, and when the external parameters change, we react accordingly. This may not seem strange, but is it really so? Once our cognition becomes solidified, it’s very difficult to change it.

For me, history belongs where it was created. We shouldn’t revisit it unless necessary. If that place disappears, history should disappear with it. Having no emotions is my best state.

Consumerism and Human Vulnerabilities

Making money by exploiting human nature. Marketers understand ordinary people too well. They can easily grasp people’s psychology and then use it to their advantage, instilling various ideas. They use moral obligation to tie feelings and money together. For example, “the first cup of milk tea,” or “your dorm leader/class monitor bought you…” The people who post these most are often merchants. Everything is for their own profit.

In daily life, we encounter many things that are convenient for us. But this so-called convenience is just a way for them to profit more quickly. The prerequisite is that you must have money; otherwise, you can’t get anywhere. So, in many cases, we should be the ones in control, not the ones being exploited. Everyone knows they need to protect themselves in society, but in reality, it’s still easy to suffer a loss and become a victim.

For example, when we meet very enthusiastic salespeople, you can easily compare their behavior to a random passerby. Is their enthusiasm real? Try telling them you don’t have any money right now. I’m certain most of them won’t give you a pleasant look. So what’s the point of emotions? I take their warm hospitality as kindness, but you’re only interested in the money in my pocket. To sell your product, you have to do the work to sell it. Therefore, their enthusiasm is just part of their job. As for us, we just pay for the goods without any extra words.

Perspectives and Awareness

Whoever thinks they are right, they are right.

You should always look at the environment and problems from various angles to form a balanced perspective.

There’s no such thing as an easy environment or a free lunch in this world. The unchanging truth is: if you choose to be comfortable, you are on the road to death.

I finally understand that there is no harm without comparison, and no motivation without pressure. I used to think I shouldn’t compare myself to others, but I overlooked a very serious problem: if I don’t compare myself, it’s hard to find the motivation to learn. The environment is so important. Almost everyone grows by comparing themselves and competing. From now on, I need to compare myself to others. For me and my current environment, it’s too comfortable. Even if I have ambition, it’s difficult to achieve anything. It’s too easy to lose myself in the world of entertainment.

The Body’s Instinct and Self-Improvement

I’m thinking about something. It seems that when we want to do something and when we actually do it, it’s not entirely decided by our will. For example, if you want to study, your brain will first outline the process. But it’s hard to get yourself to act. Your body will involuntarily wait until your rational mind tells you there’s very little time left or that you have no choice but to study. Only then will you do what you want to do.

This situation is so common that it has been integrated into our will. We automatically believe that we just don’t want to start early and want to rest as much as possible. Only when something urgent comes up do we jump up and get to work. This might be a physical instinct. After all, every move requires energy, and the less we consume, the better.

We can learn more in a shorter, high-quality period than we can in a long, inefficient one. In other words, only a small portion of our study time is effective; the rest is just filler or rest. Human bodies and energy are limited. Instead of spending a lot of time overdrawing them, we should use the time when our state is best to improve ourselves, that is, to learn. The rest of the time should be allocated reasonably. Of course, the rest of the time is for rest. The brain is a good thing. It can think of anything. A person’s body must follow a certain direction to survive. It listens to whoever’s decision will help it live. This is why early life is governed by a lower central nervous system, and maturity is governed by the brain. Who doesn’t want to get an extra benefit? Who doesn’t want to enjoy a little more?

Class Stratification and the Nature of Education

Yesterday, I watched a video set in a vocational school. A student was openly on the phone in class, disrupting the teacher, who was very angry and scolded the student. After watching the video, I was shocked. We know what vocational schools are like. They’re usually for the worst students in the county, those who can’t get into high school. We also know that schools separate generation after generation of children into different classes. The good students are together, and the poor students are together. This seems to align perfectly with social values, and everyone thinks this is right.

One of the problems is that the people who are filtered out by the state have poor knowledge and skills, so they’re not very useful to the country. The state essentially throws these people away to fend for themselves. But we also know that the environment is the most powerful influence on a person. This means that even if a student has bad grades but good character, they’ll likely lose their good character after being in a vocational school. This is because students with bad grades are basically useless to the country.

What is the country’s real goal? To select talent. For the country to grow, it needs technology and scientists in fields like mathematics and aerospace. But the people’s goal is to live a good life. They just want to live well; they’re not going to develop aerospace technology at home. To a certain extent, these two goals are at odds. But the state has power and capital, so of course, it can change this situation. It heavily supports talent-selection institutions, that is, schools. And it gradually makes schools the only path for young people. (This is also the “credit” of the masses. The state simply says, “If you study and are employed by us, you don’t have to do manual labor, and you’ll get a good salary.” This is inevitable; if you contribute to the country, it’s only right that you get some benefits.)

Using people’s weaknesses like their “intelligence” and “blindness,” it’s no surprise that everyone wants to change their fate. This has gradually become a norm, a custom: a child’s path to university is set from birth. Oh, but this is just a policy. Let’s not even discuss whether it’s a win-win.

So no matter what state the future develops into, there is one thing we must not forget: our destiny is in our own hands, not in the hands of a so-called school. No place is meant for us to stay forever. Don’t be deceived. Don’t let your eyes be blinded.

Emotional Constraints and The Pain of Memory

We will never know when, at a certain moment, thousands of memories and emotions will rush into our minds, leaving us speechless, silent, and even motionless. You know, this is the shackles of emotion. Humans cannot escape it. Life is a constant forward race. Sometimes, we also look back to see what we were like during our growth. Were we crying, screaming, or staying calm? Memory is always destined to be erased by history.

The Gig Economy and Value’s Internal Conflict

I’ve been watching TikTok a lot these days, and I’ve suddenly discovered something interesting. Video creators today know exactly what gets views and what the majority of people like to watch. So, they make videos like that to get more views. As a result, many types of videos now share a commonality: they feature women. I don’t know if this is because of our male counterparts’ preferences. For example, one genre on TikTok is about building and reviewing computers. Computer science isn’t typically a female-dominated field; most are men. But later, influenced by viewership, women started entering the industry—or rather, men prefer to see beautiful women next to computers. The women in these videos are often pretty, even wearing JK sailor uniforms that many men love.

So this has gradually become the internal competition of the industry. The consequences of this trend are clear. This external, easily imitable value will eventually wear out the creators. But the viewers get to enjoy the show. I can predict that in the future, anything that is in demand will be walking on the edge of the law. Ha!

Political Interests and Human Needs

Broadly speaking, interests can be divided into economic, ecological, social, public, cultural, and political. Taking political interests as an example, in addition to money, the public also needs power and rights. After meeting their basic survival needs, people want a more dignified life with more “face,” which is the pursuit of rights. Power and rights together form political interests. In fact, public power and private power are the two core keywords in politics.

We will protect others after we meet our basic needs (the standards for material and spiritual needs vary for each person; smarter people tend to lean toward the latter because they can obtain material benefits more easily). This also explains why personal ability is so important—it determines your level of social contribution and moral integrity. How can a person who can’t even protect themselves genuinely talk about love? Some people sacrifice material benefits for spiritual ones, while others do the opposite.

The struggle with the outside world (everything beyond ourselves) that arises from our survival instincts damages our social nature. Our spiritual world becomes inclined towards the Asura path—loneliness, vigilance, loss of trust and security, sleeplessness, a knife under the pillow—and eventually forces us to lose the will to live.

The Capital Trap and the “Worker’s” Plight

The basic form of a typical worker (i.e., the simplest mass thinking) is: I work to get money, and then I use that money to buy what I want, living a stable life. For these people, it’s hard to be free in a capitalist society. Their simple minds make them easy to exploit. Under normal circumstances, bosses can easily buy their freedom with more money. These people often work in common positions that are indispensable but in high demand. Through bribery, they can easily be forced to work, and they often have no savings. This is not only because of their low wages but also because of various social诱导. Things like “your first device,” brainwashing ads, and exploiting people’s psychological desires—their simple thoughts have been firmly grasped by big data.

The only goal of society is to get money from you, forcing you to work for a living. This is a vicious cycle that is the reality for most young people today. In short, society drains your money in various ways, and you are forced to work for society. And what does the “victim” get? Eating instant noodles all day for an iPhone? For a luxury bag? To show off on WeChat Moments that they’ve been to Starbucks? In short, it’s all to satisfy those small desires.

Staying Clear-Headed and Realistic

I strongly agree with the idea of not interfering in other people’s lives. Everyone has their own path—for love, for feelings, for ideals. What we think is right is only what we think is right. If everyone judged others based on their own knowledge, it would only get worse. So there’s no need to judge them. Just watch them silently and do your own thing.

Staying clear-headed is essential for succeeding in society, but the price of it is something not everyone can bear. For people, the simplest and most basic need is to live a good life. But this is said before we know the truth. When have people’s needs ever been satisfied? If you weren’t forced, would you say such a thing? People’s needs are never-ending. Once they get a little, they want more. Isn’t this one of humanity’s flaws? Of course, this is how society develops. No matter the process, it’s maintained in a relatively stable state. As long as there’s a benefit, everyone can be treated equally. As long as you have a benefit, we can be friends.

No one can say what this society is like. People who say such things are powerless and weak. This society is a huge platform for competition. Just as history is always written by the victors, whether it’s a person or a country, who doesn’t want to say a few nice things about themselves and spit on the losers? This society is always competing, and there is always a loser. Losing isn’t scary. The scary part is admitting defeat!

Another autumn is coming soon, and the weather is getting cooler. It’s that season again. Whenever it comes, my mood gets very low. Gloomy skies, a cold world.

In daily life, we should not help others without thinking. We should first analyze whether a person is worth helping. Some people are grateful after you help them, while others are like a bottomless pit. Not only can you never fill it, but you’ll eventually realize you were foolish, as they took your help for granted. Since everyone has different thoughts, and those thoughts are supported by a different level of understanding, we cannot say that the latter’s behavior is bad or unchangeable. Maybe one day in the future, they will realize it. But at least for now, those who take advantage without giving anything in return won’t have many people willing to live with them.

Don’t get angry easily. Analyze whether the situation is worth getting angry over. If you’re walking down the street and a hooligan curses at you, do you have to get angry and curse back? While “doing unto others as they have done unto you” might be suitable for people who aren’t ordinary—people who will know what you mean with just a hint—you should never play a lute to an ox. Good methods should be used on people who understand them. Don’t act self-righteous among ordinary people. What’s the point of being self-righteous? You’ll only be discriminated against.

The Meaning of Life and the “I’m Willing” Fallacy

No matter what your status, there’s always more passivity than proactivity. Everything we do, we say is for ourselves, but in the end, it’s for others. No matter how big your house is, you can only live in it for one lifetime. No matter how good your car is, you’ll just fix it when it breaks. A bungalow is a home, and a villa is a home. A bicycle can run, and a luxury car can run. What’s the difference?

Some people live on the edge, taking risks their whole lives, living a tense life. They feel this life is meaningful. Others live a peaceful life, with three meals a day, marriage, children, and a funeral. They also feel their life is meaningful. So what exactly is a meaningful life? The answer is: whoever feels their life is meaningful, that is the most meaningful life. No matter what the system, environmental conditions, or standard of living, everything that can be debated loses to a person’s simple phrase, “I’m willing.” Yes, if everyone is “willing,” then what’s fair or unfair, what’s cruel or not cruel? What reason is there to change them?

The Illusion of Friendship and The “Skirt Effect”

Actually, except for the genuine feelings of your own family, most of the feelings you encounter in society are fake. Someone might ask, “If that’s the case, why do I always struggle to talk with my parents but have a great time with my friends?” Doesn’t this contradict what you said? Someone once explained this phenomenon, saying that parents’ love is unconditional, while friendships are conditional. Others say that the reason we show a lack of love toward our parents is precisely because we know their love for us is unconditional, so we often choose to ignore and waste it. But friends are different. At the beginning, we don’t know each other well, so we don’t know if the other person is being sincere. Therefore, many people cherish the feelings given by their friends. Hehe.

We always want to face the world with a smile and show our best side. But in the end, we find that the kinder we are, the more likely others are to see us as easy to bully and to order us around. In my opinion, for anyone to show their beauty, they must first have the condition of being able to protect themselves. Only then can they better show themselves. Of course, thinking deeper, is there absolute protection in this society? Maybe there are a few isolated examples. I think most people get some minor injuries during the process of being protected, and in exchange, they don’t suffer major harm or they exchange their freedom for future stability. This is the law of society. Either someone is willing to suffer a loss, or everyone profits equally, or someone takes all the profits. If everyone in this society refused to suffer a loss, it would not be stable. So, again, our own happiness is built on our own or others’ pain. This “we” includes all living things on Earth, because you might be harming someone, like eating a pig or a crawfish. Everyone knows what the current world is like. “Telecom fraud” is an example. More and more scams are emerging, and from a profit-driven perspective, they don’t care about the consequences for others. For adults, the impact may not be that big, because their awareness is higher. True friendship is seen in times of trouble; a person’s heart is seen in a major event. I think this is what human society is. It’s not strange at all. So, again, the world is vast, and all kinds of people exist. At the very least, when we meet them, we shouldn’t be flustered or scared. Just know when to attach yourself and when to keep your distance.

The “Skirt Effect” and Adaptation

The role of women in human society is truly strange. Didn’t someone mention the “skirt effect”? It says that during a certain period, the shorter women’s skirts are, the higher the economic level of that society, and vice versa. In short, the world is balanced. The appearance of any phenomenon must have a cause, at least under normal development.

Many people probably think that the more easily a person can adapt to society, the better they will live. But, on the other hand, does this mean they are more easily changed from who they originally were? This is a frightening thought because whether these people succeed depends entirely on their environment. The outcome for a child born in a terrible environment will definitely not be the same as for a child from a family of scholars. And if a child’s personality is stubborn in childhood, isn’t that also a form of self-protection?
